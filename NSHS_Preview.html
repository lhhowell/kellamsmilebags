A student-driven service initiative focused on spreading kindness and meeting community needs in meaningful ways. Through this work, technology becomes a pathway for empathy, connection, and positive social impact.

🌍

At Kellam High School in Virginia Beach, Virginia, students are building Smile Bags. Items in the bag are student-made, intentionally designed, and built around one shared word.

At the heart of Smile Bags are 25 OneWords — connecting environmental science to social emotional well-being.

Each of the 25 OneWords lives in two worlds at once — in the natural systems students study, and in the inner lives students navigate every day. From Absorb to Transform, these words remind us that the same forces that sustain ecosystems — balance, flow, renewal, resilience — are the forces that sustain people too.

Who's Building the Bags

Each participating class contributes at least one student-made item to every smile bag. A sneak preview of select materials is available below.

Smile Bag Guidelines

All smile bag items must be student-made. The only acceptable purchased items are crayons, colored pencils, paint kits, and Play-Doh.

✦ New items will be added as we bring on more participants.

Who Receives a Smile Bag

A bag finds its way to a student
who needs it most.

Smile Bags are distributed across Kellam's eight feeder schools and Kellam High School itself. Each school receives ten bags. Guidance counselors identify students who may need encouragement, motivation, or simply a reason to smile — and a bag finds its way to them at exactly the right moment.

Every item inside was made by a Kellam student who didn't know your name — but made something just for you.
