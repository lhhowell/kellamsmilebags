At Kellam High School in Virginia Beach, Virginia, students are building Smile Bags. Items in the bag are student-made, intentionally designed, and built around one shared word.

At the heart of Smile Bags are 25 OneWords — connecting environmental science to social emotional well-being.

A student-driven service initiative focused on spreading kindness and meeting community needs in meaningful ways. Through this work, technology becomes a pathway for empathy, connection, and positive social impact.

At the heart of Smile Bags are 25 OneWords — a shared set of words that connect environmental science concepts to social emotional well-being. Each word was chosen because it lives in two worlds at once: in the natural systems students study, and in the inner lives students navigate every day. From Absorb to Transform, these words remind us that the same forces that sustain ecosystems — balance, flow, renewal, resilience — are the forces that sustain people too.

Who Receives a Smile Bag

A bag finds its way to a student
who needs it most.

Smile Bags are distributed across Kellam's eight feeder schools and Kellam High School itself — ten bags per school, ninety students reached. Guidance counselors identify students who may need encouragement, motivation, or simply a reason to smile. A bag is chosen for them deliberately, not randomly — at exactly the right moment.

Every item inside was made by a Kellam student who didn't know your name — but made something just for you.

● Instructional Technology

Smile Bags Mascot Sticker, Hidden Picture Activities & OneWord DNA System

Laura Howell · Instructional Technology Specialist · Smile Bags Signature Design · Word & Wonder Founder

The Instructional Technology Specialist designed the official Smile Bags mascot sticker and 25 OneWord hidden picture activities — one for each of the project's 25 shared words. The mascot serves as the signature identity of the project, appearing on documents, the website, and as a sticker placed in every smile bag. The ITS also invented the OneWord DNA System — a one-of-a-kind music activity called Compose It! where each OneWord's letters become musical notes, giving every word its own unique Voice for students to hear and build upon. The OneWord Trading Card Kiosk lets any student select a word, choose their grade level, and print a stunning inspirational card-style collectible card paired with an inspirational quote — ready to keep, trade, or tuck into a Smile Bag. The OneWord Coloring Book showcases 225 AI-generated coloring pages across 9 distinct art styles — scenic, cartoon, woodcut, artistic, K–4 icon, nature poster, fine line botanical, nature journal, and modern line — created across ChatGPT, CoPilot, and Gemini — with the Secondary Hub's 225 game activities architected and built in partnership with Claude. Word World: The OneWord Journey brings all 25 words to life through original stories, illustrated group scenes, hidden picture activities, coloring pages, and an interactive Secondary Hub — all in one experience.

● Open to All Contributors · Independent Pathways

Crafting Smiles Hub

Open to all contributors · No class assignment required · Hub coordinated by Laura Howell, ITS

The Crafting Smiles Hub is the home for independent contributions — open to current students, alumni, former STE(A)M campers, and friends of the project. No class required, no graduation date required. Seven pathways live under this one roof: design a OneWord 3D printed bookmark, build a Magic Illusion Spinner, choose a coloring page from the ABC’s Animals or Fruits & Veggies coloring books, build a complete 10-item Smile Bag through Science National Honor Society, earn club service points across multiple kiosks, or contribute an Original 3D Design — a maker pathway for alumni, former STE(A)M campers, and friends bringing their own tools and craft. Every path connects to the 25 shared OneWords — one word, one child, one act of kindness. Future Ready: Problem Solvers & Value Creators; Personally & Socially Responsible; Resilient Learners.
