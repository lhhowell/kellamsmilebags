Activity Kiosk

Your guide to building a complete 8-part Smile Bag package — six activities, a personal letter, and a custom sticker — all connected to your OneWord.

Ms. Marquand · Grades 9–12
Spring 2026
🌟

What You Are Doing. You are creating a personal package for a real child in your community who needs a smile. Using the Leadership Activity Kiosk, you will build six activities, write a personal letter by hand, and design a 2-inch OneWord sticker — all connected to your word. Your package will be placed in a Smile Bag.

Getting Ready
Before You Start
  • Know your OneWord — your teacher will assign it.
  • Know who you are designing for — Elementary 3–5, Middle 6–8, or High School 9–12.
  • Open the kiosk using the link your teacher posts on Canvas.
  • Type your name in the name box before selecting your word.
The Package
Your Eight Activities
Tab 01
🤝 Friendship Cards
Four challenges a child can do with a friend. See the example first, then make your own.
Tab 02
🧩 Puzzle It Out
A riddle with a surprise answer that connects to your word. Write your own version.
Tab 03
💛 Kindness Coupons
Four coupons for real things you'd do for someone. Make them genuine.
Tab 04
🌬️ Calm-Down Toolkit
A breathing technique named after your word. Try the breathing circle — it actually works!
Tab 05
✨ Dream Jar
A fill-in activity about dreams and goals, written for the child who gets your bag.
Tab 06
💡 Big Idea Planner
A step-by-step plan inspired by your word. 3–5 steps depending on the age group.
Tab 07 · Special
✉️ Letter
A personal letter written by you, to the child. Guided by a tutor. Written by hand on cardstock.
Tab 08 · Special
🏷️ Sticker
A 2-inch round sticker with your word, emoji, and a short phrase. Your teacher prints these for the class.
Tabs 1–6
How to Complete the Activities
1
Click the tab at the top.
Each tab is one of your eight activities. Start with Tab 01 and work across.
2
Click See the Example first.
Read it carefully. It shows what a good version looks like for your OneWord and age group.
3
Click Make Your Own.
Fill in every field thoughtfully. Don't rush — a real child will use this.
4
Check your work.
Does it connect to your word? Is it right for the age group you chose?
5
Click the Print button for that tab.
Print it. Set it aside.
6
Repeat for all six tabs.
Tabs 01 through 06 follow the same pattern. Tabs 07 and 08 are different — see below.
Tab 7
Writing Your Letter
⚠️ Important

Your letter must be approved by your teacher before you can print the writing guide. Do not skip any steps.

1
Answer the 5 tutor questions
The kiosk will ask you 5 questions one at a time. Answer each one in your own words. Short answers will be sent back — aim for at least a full sentence each time.
2
Use Gemini if you want help
Click Open in Gemini to open the Gemini writing tutor in a new tab. It will ask you the same 5 questions and help you shape your words into a letter. Copy your finished letter and paste it back into the kiosk.
3
Review and edit your letter
Read your draft carefully. Does it sound like YOU? Make any changes you want in the editor.
4
Submit for teacher review
Click Submit for Teacher Review. Your teacher will receive an email with your letter and approve it.
5
Write down your token code!
A code like KSB-4F2A will appear on screen. WRITE IT DOWN. If you close this browser, you can enter the code when you come back to check whether your letter has been approved.
6
Wait for approval
The kiosk checks for approval automatically every 20 seconds for 15 minutes. When approved, the Print Writing Guide button will unlock.
7
Print and write by hand
Click Print Writing Guide. A new window opens with your Gemini draft at the top (for reference) and blank handwriting lines below. Write your letter by hand on this cardstock sheet. This is what goes in the Smile Bag.
💡 If You Close the Browser

No problem. Open the kiosk again, go to Tab 7, and enter your token code in the "Already Submitted? Resume Here" box. The kiosk will check whether you have been approved.

Tab 8
Designing Your Sticker

The sticker tab is the final step. Your teacher will print all stickers together as one class set — you do NOT print your sticker yourself.

  • Choose your sticker emoji from the grid.
  • Choose your sticker color.
  • Type a short phrase (up to 30 characters). Ideas: "Kellam Smile Bags · 2026" or your word's meaning.
  • Watch the live 2-inch circle preview update as you design.
  • Click Submit My Sticker Design when you are happy with it.
  • Done! Your teacher will print it with the rest of the class.
