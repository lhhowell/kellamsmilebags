🔬 AP Biology
Class Assignment

AP Biology students connect their Norfolk Zoo capstone project to their Smile Bag. This counts as a class grade — not service points. Each student produces 10 items rooted in their Zoo Project observations, science, and empathy for a K–5 student.

💕 T.I.D.E. Coalition
Club Service Project

T.I.D.E. Coalition members build their bags as a club service project — no service points. Their items focus on belonging, inclusion, and wellness. Their bags carry the T.I.D.E. values of Togetherness, Inclusion, Diversity, and Equity into every elementary classroom they reach.

Zoo Project → Bag Item Translation

Each AP Biology Zoo Project option translates into a specific bag item. Students use their field observations, research, and zoo work as the foundation for their science contribution to the bag.

Every Bag
5 Core Required Items

Every bag — AP Biology and T.I.D.E. — must include these 5 items. They ensure every elementary student receives a complete, consistent experience regardless of who made the bag.

  • 1
    Zoo Project Bag Item
    AP Bio only. Translate the Zoo Project into one physical item using the translation chart above. T.I.D.E. students substitute a belonging activity card from the Belonging Activity Builder kiosk.
  • 2
    Elementary Science Supply Bar + Bookmark
    Visit the Elementary Science Supply Bar and choose 1–2 hands-on supplies. Use the Bookmark Builder Kiosk to design 1 bookmark connecting the supply to the student’s OneWord. Bookmark + supplies go in the bag together.
  • 3
    Binary Coding or Cipher Activity
    Design an original coding or decoding activity using the Binary Coding Kiosk, Secret Cipher Kiosk, or Color-by-Code Kiosk. Must be completable with crayons or a pencil by a K–5 student.
  • 4
    OneWord Handwritten Letter
    A warm, personal letter to the elementary student. Written on a 5×7 card or quality paper. Signed with first name only. This is the only handwritten item permitted in the bag.
  • 5
    OneWord Belonging Card
    Use the Belonging Activity Builder to design an affirmation card, belonging prompt, kindness challenge, or common ground activity. Print the 1-up card for the bag — or use the 12-up sheet to produce extras for other bags.

Workflow
Student Step-by-Step

Both AP Biology and T.I.D.E. students follow the same general workflow. Direct them to the Student Guide for full details.

  • 1
    Visit the Overview Site
    Read the mission, understand the recipients, review who gets what. APBio_TIDE_Preview.html
  • 2
    Read the Student Guide
    Review the Zoo Project translation chart, 5 core items, and kiosk instructions. APBio_TIDE_Student_Guide.html
  • 3
    Choose a OneWord
    Students select one of the 25 shared OneWords. All items in their bag connect back to this word.
  • 4
    Build the 5 Core Items
    Zoo Project item, Science Supply Bar + Bookmark, Binary/Cipher activity, Handwritten letter, Belonging card.
  • 5
    Choose 5 More from the Kiosk Menu
    Browse the 20-item kiosk menu and select 5 additional items across at least 3 categories. APBio_TIDE_Kiosk_Menu.html
  • 6
    Print, Assemble & Submit
    Print all digital items at the designated print station. Assemble the bag. Submit to teacher for review before delivery.

Logistics
Suggested Timeline
Phase 1 — Launch
Introduce the Project
Share the Overview site with students. Walk through the Student Guide together. Assign or allow students to choose their OneWord. AP Bio students identify their Zoo Project option and corresponding bag item.
Phase 2 — Build
Kiosk Work Sessions
Students work at kiosk stations to design, generate, and print their 10 items. Teacher circulates to review content for age-appropriateness before printing. Handwritten letters are completed last.
Phase 3 — Review
Teacher Quality Check
Review each completed bag before it is sealed. Check that all 10 items are present, content is K–5 appropriate, and the handwritten letter is warm and encouraging. Return any items that need revision.
Phase 4 — Deliver
Assembly & Delivery by May 30, 2026
Completed bags are delivered to Laura Howell, ITS for final review and coordination with North Landing Elementary. Guidance counselors identify recipients and deliver bags at the right moment for each child.
Printing
What Gets Printed

All kiosk-generated items are designed to print on standard 8.5×11 paper unless noted. Here are the recommended print settings for each item type.

Default print size: All activity sheets print at 8.5×11. Cut to 5×7 after printing if needed for the bag.
  • 📄
    Binary / Cipher / Color-by-Code sheets
    Print full page, B&W is fine. Cut to 5×7 after printing. Laminate optional.
  • 📄
    Bookmark
    Print full page, cut to bookmark size (approx 2×7 inches). Laminate recommended.
  • 📄
    Belonging Card (1-up)
    Print full page, trim to card size. Heavy cardstock recommended.
  • 📄
    Belonging Card (12-up)
    Prints 12 identical cards per sheet. Cut apart for use across multiple bags. Universal — no school name, shareable across all recipient schools.
  • 📄
    Food Web / Anatomy Match cut-and-sort
    Print full page, B&W. Elementary student cuts and sorts the pieces as the activity.
