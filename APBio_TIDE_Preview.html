Who Receives a Smile Bag

Somewhere, an elementary student
is going to open your bag.
Make it matter.

Smile Bags are distributed to North Landing Elementary through Kellam's eight feeder schools and Kellam High School. Guidance counselors identify students who may need encouragement, motivation, or simply a reason to smile. Every item inside was made by a high school student who didn't know their name — but made something just for them.

Who's Building the Bags

Two Groups, One Mission

AP Biology students bring scientific depth from their Norfolk Zoo field trip. T.I.D.E. Coalition members bring a commitment to belonging, inclusion, and student well-being. Together they build 20 elementary bags — 10 each — for North Landing Elementary students.

🔬 AP Biology

AP Biology

AP Biology students connect their Norfolk Zoo field trip capstone project to real, physical bag items for elementary students. Their zoo observations — behavioral journals, food webs, conservation campaigns — become hands-on activities designed for a K–5 recipient.

Sponsor: Elena O'Brien · Elementary Edition
💕 T.I.D.E. Coalition

T.I.D.E. Coalition

Togetherness through Inclusion, Diversity, and Equity. TIDE members build bags centered on wellness, belonging, and joy — creating items that help an elementary student feel seen, encouraged, and cared for by someone who didn't know their name but made something just for them.

Sponsor: Elena O'Brien · Elementary Edition
