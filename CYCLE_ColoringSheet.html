CYCLE — Vocabulary Coloring Sheet
Kellam Smile Bags · Kellam High School · Spring 2026
CATERPILLAR
A young butterfly or moth in its crawling stage, eating and growing before it transforms into something new.
CHRYSALIS
The protective shell a caterpillar forms around itself while it changes into a butterfly — a symbol of transformation.
BUTTERFLY
A winged insect that begins as a caterpillar — proof that incredible change is possible.
LEAF
A part of a plant that captures sunlight to make food — and falls each autumn to nourish the soil for new growth.
FALLEN LOG
A tree that has ended one life and begun another — becoming a home for mushrooms, insects, and new plants.
CYCLE
A series of events that repeat in order — like seasons, water, or life itself always returning to a beginning.
WATER CYCLE
Water evaporates, forms clouds, falls as rain, and flows back to the sea — always moving, never lost.
SPROUT
A tiny new plant breaking through the soil — the first sign that something new is growing even when you can't see it yet.
WHEEL
A circle that keeps turning — like the cycles of nature, it never stops and always comes back around.
SUN
The source of energy for almost every cycle on Earth — rising and setting each day, powering life through seasons and years.
