Who Receives Your Bag

Somewhere, a student is having a hard day.
Your bag is going to find them.

Smile Bags are distributed across Kellam's eight feeder schools and Kellam High School itself — ten bags per school. Guidance counselors identify students who may need encouragement, motivation, or simply a reason to smile. A bag is chosen for them deliberately, not randomly — at exactly the right moment.

You will never meet the student who receives your bag. But every item you create — every choice you make about what to include and how to make it — is an act of care for someone real. That's what makes this different from a class project. This one matters beyond the grade.

How It Works

You will build one complete Smile Bag for a middle school student in grades 6–8. Every item in the bag must be an original creation — something you designed and made using technology. The only exception is one handwritten inspirational letter. Your sponsor approves your participation, verifies each item, and determines the final service point value. The 5-point guideline is a starting point — your sponsor has the option to adjust points up or down based on their chapter's requirements.

Science Supply Bar + Bookmark Builder Kiosk
Visit the Science Supply Bar and choose 1 or 2 supplies. Use the Bookmark Builder Kiosk to design 1 bookmark that connects your supply choice to your OneWord through scientific reasoning and vocabulary. Your bookmark + your supplies go into the bag together. If a supply you want is not on the bar, your sponsor must approve it in advance.
Required · Core · Bookmark + 1–2 Supplies

Category 2
Design & Print
2
Original Digitally Designed Visual Item
Design an original visual item using Canva, Google Drawings, Adobe Express, or a similar digital tool. Print at 5×7 size. The design must be entirely your own — not a template, not a kiosk output. Choose one from the options below:
  • 🌀 Mandala or zentangle template — digitally designed with instructions for the recipient
  • 🕐 Zentangle or doodle prompt sheet — digitally framed with step-by-step doodle instructions
  • 🃏 Mini sticker sheet — designed digitally, printed on sticker paper, cut to size
  • 📖 Mini zine or folded booklet — 8-panel single sheet, digitally designed and folded
  • 🌸 Nature-themed paper craft — digitally designed, printed, fold-and-cut assembly
Required · Core · Print at 5×7
🔧
Category 3
Construct & Build
3
Original 3D Printed Design — Tinkercad Required
Design an original 3D object in Tinkercad. This item must be entirely your own design — not a downloaded model, not a template. The design should connect meaningfully to your OneWord or to the calming, science-themed purpose of the bag. When your design is ready, email your shareable Tinkercad link to lhhowell@vbschools.com and it will be printed in D210.
🆕 Bonus Option — Zero Points

If you prefer to use a kiosk template instead of designing your own, you may create one of the following: a 3D bookmark, keychain, or spinner. You keep one for yourself and one goes in the bag. This option does not count toward your 10 items but is a great way to contribute if you want to participate beyond your bag.

Required · Email Tinkercad Link to lhhowell@vbschools.com

Words & Wellness

Original Digitally Designed Wellness Item
Create a digitally designed item focused on calm, encouragement, or well-being. This could be a breathing or grounding exercise card, a calm corner guide, a set of affirmation cards, or a similar wellness-focused item. It must be your own design, printed and ready to place in the bag.
Required · Core
✍️
Handwritten Exception — One Only
Inspirational Letter
5
Handwritten Inspirational Letter
This is the only handwritten item permitted in your bag. Write a personal, encouraging letter to the middle school student who will receive your bag. Your letter should feel warm, genuine, and connected to your OneWord. It does not need to be long — it needs to be real. Written on a 5×7 card, cardstock, or quality paper. No typed letters — handwritten only for this item. Sign with your first name only.
Handwritten · One Item Only · 5×7 · First Name Only
Part Two
Additional Items — Choose 5 from the Creation Kiosk

Your remaining 5 items come from the SNHS Creation Kiosk Menu. Browse all four categories and choose any 5 items that aren't already covered in your core items above. Aim for variety — your bag should feel like a thoughtful collection, not repetitions of the same idea.

Items #10 (Design & Print) and #14 (Construct & Build) on the kiosk menu are student choice — bring your idea to your sponsor before you begin. Item #11 (3D Print) requires emailing your Tinkercad link to lhhowell@vbschools.com before printing.
