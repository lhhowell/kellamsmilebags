AVID Quote Card
Teacher Guide
Deena Vinson · AVID · Grades 9–12 · Kellam High School · Spring 2026

AVID students create original inspirational quote cards for children receiving Smile Bags. Each student selects one of the 25 shared OneWords, completes a Quick Write reflection using Costa's Levels of Thinking, works with a Gemini AI writing tutor to refine their quote, and designs a 4-inch card in their chosen colors and font. Cards are printed and cut on cardstock and placed in the smile bags.

Materials & Setup

What You Need

  • Chromebooks or laptops with internet access (1 per student)
  • AVID Quote Card Kiosk: lhhowell.github.io/kellamsmilebags/AVID_QuoteCard_Kiosk.html
  • Gemini Writing Tutor Gem link shared with students (see Gemini Gem Setup Guide)
  • Cardstock for printing — letter size, any color
  • Cricut machine or scissors for cutting circle cards
  • Google Sheet — AVID Quote Card Submissions — to monitor submissions in real time

Before the Activity

Set up the Gemini Gem using the separate Gemini Gem Setup Guide.
Paste the Gem link into the kiosk HTML where it says GEM_LINK_HERE, then re-upload to GitHub.
Share the kiosk link with students via Google Classroom or project on screen.
Post the Gem link in Google Classroom or display as a QR code.
Open the AVID Quote Card Submissions Google Sheet to monitor in real time.

Activity Flow — 8 Steps

StepWhat Happens
Step 1 — ShapeStudents choose Square (4″ × 4″) or Circle (4″ round). Circle cards are cut with the Cricut.
Step 2 — Name & GradeStudents enter their first name and grade. The name appears on the printed card.
Step 3 — OneWordStudents choose one of 25 shared OneWords. Each word shows its meaning to help students connect.
Step 4 — ReflectAVID strategy step. Students complete a Costa's Quick Write (2–3 sentences), then open the Gemini Writing Tutor. The tutor uses Socratic questioning to deepen thinking before they write their quote.
Step 5 — Write QuoteStudents enter their final 120-character quote — refined through the Gemini tutor conversation.
Step 6 — Color PairStudents choose one of 7 color pairs. Color 1 = card background. Color 2 = text and border.
Step 7 — Font StyleStudents choose from 4 font styles: Elegant, Friendly, Script, or Bold.
Step 8 — Preview & SubmitStudents see a live preview of their finished card. They submit and receive a KSB-XXXX token code as their receipt.

Printing & Production

🖨️ Printing

  • Open AVID Quote Card Submissions Sheet after class
  • Each row contains all design data: word, quote, shape, color pair, font, name
  • Print directly from the kiosk SVG preview, or recreate in Canva / Google Slides
  • Print on cardstock, letter size, color printer

✂️ Cutting

  • Square cards: trim to 4″ × 4″ with paper cutter
  • Circle cards: cut with Cricut using 4″ circle template
  • No design file needed — just set cut to 4″ circle
  • Optional: laminate for durability

Teacher Tips

Pair-share option: Before students open the Gemini tutor, have them share their Quick Write with a partner. This mirrors the Socratic seminar structure AVID students know.
Model the process: Do a live demo with your own OneWord before students start. Show them what a Level 3 quote looks like versus a Level 1 quote.
Vocabulary connection: The 25 OneWords are all environmental science terms. Consider connecting to Environmental Science content or current events.
Token codes: Each student receives a KSB-XXXX token code after submitting. This serves as their submission receipt and can be used to verify completion.
Monitoring: The Google Sheet updates in real time. You can monitor who has submitted during class.
