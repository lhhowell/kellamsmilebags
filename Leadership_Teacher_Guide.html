01

Unit Overview

Leadership students build a complete six-part Smile Bag package for a child recipient (elementary, middle, or high school). Each student's package is connected to a single OneWord — chosen with intention — that threads through all six pieces: five interactive kiosks plus a handwritten letter reviewed by you before it goes in the bag.

Every kiosk follows the same flow: pick a OneWord → pick a category/theme set → pick an option → render a card. Students screenshot the card and upload it to the matching shared Drive folder. The tracker counts uploads automatically.

The six pieces students build:

  • Puzzle It Out — a riddle tied to the OneWord
  • Kindness Coupons — four redeemable acts of service
  • Calm-Down Toolkit — a breathing technique named after the OneWord
  • Dream Jar — a guided dream + goal + self-message
  • Big Idea Planner — a structured project plan for a young person
  • Handwritten Letter — a personal letter, teacher-approved before bagging
🌟 Future Ready Attributes

This unit directly supports Personally & Socially Responsible (students produce real artifacts for real recipients), Communicators (letter and card writing to unknown audiences), and Collaborators (shared class progress, classroom-wide package assembly).

Learning Standards

Leadership Skills connects directly to multiple Virginia SOL and national leadership/CTE frameworks. Standards references below are suggested alignments; adapt as needed.

VA SOL · Leadership 5.3
Service Learning. The entire Smile Bags project is a structured service-learning experience — identifying a community need, designing a response, executing the plan, and reflecting on impact.
VA SOL · Leadership 2.1
Communication Skills. The Handwritten Letter requires students to write to an unknown, younger audience with warmth, specificity, and age-appropriate voice. Scaffolded by age-track prompts in the Letter Kiosk.
VA SOL · Leadership 4.2
Goal Setting & Planning. The Big Idea Planner asks students to scaffold real project planning (purpose, steps, resources) for a child just starting out — a meta-level leadership exercise in structured thinking.
CASEL · Social Awareness
Perspective-Taking & Empathy. Every kiosk requires students to imagine a specific recipient and tailor the content to that child's age, context, and needs — the core of CASEL's social awareness competency.
CASEL · Responsible Decision-Making
Ethical Responsibility. The Letter Teacher Gate is itself a decision-making lesson — students understand that what goes to a child carries weight, and their work is reviewed before it reaches its recipient.
ISTE · 1.4
Innovative Designer. Students use digital tools (kiosks, Google Drive, Gemini AI reflection partner) to create original artifacts within constraints and for a real audience.

Tab 06 · Special
Handwritten Letter
1 letter per student · Teacher Gate

The only kiosk with a teacher-review step. Student picks an age track (Elementary / Middle / High) with a starter prompt and 2–3 guiding questions, handwrites the letter on a Smile Bags letterhead panel (4–7 sentences), and hands the physical letter to you before it goes in the bag.

📝 Printable Letterhead

Letters are written on the official Smile Bags 2026 Letterhead → — two letters per landscape page with a dashed cut line between them. Print enough sheets for the whole class, cut along the dashed lines, and distribute one half-sheet per student as they reach this kiosk.

The Teacher Gate Workflow

  1. Student picks an age track and prompt. The Letter Kiosk shows 3 age cards with starter prompts and guiding questions. Student picks the one that fits their recipient.
  2. Student writes by hand on a Smile Bags letterhead panel. Using the prompt as scaffold. Length: 4–7 sentences. Tone: warm, specific, positive. Voice: their own. Students sign on the "Signature" line; the pre-printed footer identifies their class and you as their teacher.
  3. Student hands the card to you. The kiosk reminds them to check legibility, tone, signature, and fit before presenting.
  4. You approve or ask for a rewrite. Once approved, the letter joins the bag. No Drive upload needed — the physical card itself is the artifact.

05

Viewing Submissions

Leadership uses a Drive-upload tracking pattern (distinct from other classes' direct-submission kiosks). Students screenshot their kiosk output and drop it into the matching Drive folder. A live tracker counts uploads per folder and displays class-wide progress on the menu page.

📂 One-Time Setup (Do This First)

Before students start working, confirm the shared parent folder Leadership Smile Bags · Spring 2026 contains six child folders with these exact names:

  • Kindness Coupons
  • Dream Jar
  • Calm-Down Toolkit
  • Puzzle It Out
  • Big Idea Planner
  • Handwritten Letter

Names must match exactly (spelling, spacing, hyphens). The tracker auto-discovers folders by name — one typo anywhere means that tile shows — instead of counting.

How to view progress

The Leadership Menu itself shows class-wide progress in real time — open Leadership_Menu.html on the projector at the start of each work session. Students see the tally tick up live as their classmates upload.

Direct links:

  • Leadership Smile Bags Drive Folder — where all student uploads live
  • Leadership Tracker Web App — direct tracker endpoint (paste into browser for a JSON count)

The Student Upload Workflow

  1. Student completes a kiosk — their card renders on screen.
  2. Student takes a screenshot (Chromebook: Ctrl+Show Windows, or Ctrl+Shift+4 on Mac).
  3. Student clicks Drive Folder on the kiosk header — jumps to the parent folder.
  4. Student opens the matching child folder and drags the screenshot in.
  5. Within ~60 seconds, the tracker counts the upload and the tile ticks up.
⚠️ Troubleshooting: a tile shows — instead of a count

The Drive folder name doesn't match what the tracker expects. Open the parent folder, check that the child folder name matches the setup list above exactly (case, spaces, hyphens), and rename if needed. The tile populates within 60 seconds.

Bag Assembly (When A Student Is Done)

When a student has uploaded all 5 kiosk screenshots AND handed you an approved letter, their package is ready:

  • Print each student's 5 screenshots from their matching Drive folders (4×6 or 5×7 format).
  • Cut, fold, or trim cards as the format requires (Kindness Coupons cut along dashed lines).
  • Add the approved Handwritten Letter to the stack.
  • Place all 6 items in a Smile Bag along with any physical extras (snack, small toy, sticker).
  • Tag the bag with the recipient's age range so the right bag reaches the right child.

The Gemini Gem Partner

A custom Gemini AI reflection partner built for this project. Students can use it for a 10–15 minute 1-on-1 conversation that helps them explore what their OneWord means in their own life — and, if they choose, how it connects to the kind of leader they're becoming. It is completely optional, but some students find it genuinely valuable before completing the final Reflection Form.

🔗 Gem Link

⚠️ Verify VBCPS Access First

Before offering this to students, verify Gemini Gem access on a student Chromebook. VBCPS has been actively adjusting Gemini availability for student accounts, and a Gem that works for you may not work for students. Have one student open the link in their school-logged-in Chrome — if they see the chat interface and can type, students have access. If they see an authentication wall or "no access" message, treat the Gem as a teacher-only demo tool or skip this step.

Assessment Rubric

Suggested assessment criteria. Adapt weights to fit your grading system. Because every kiosk upload is automatically counted by the tracker, completion is quantifiable without any self-reporting.

Dimension Proficient (4) Developing (3) Emerging (2)
Completion All 6 package items complete (5 kiosk uploads in matching Drive folders + approved handwritten letter) 4–5 items complete 3 or fewer items complete
OneWord Integrity The chosen OneWord threads consistently through all 6 pieces; connections are intentional, not surface-level OneWord is present in most pieces but feels attached rather than integrated OneWord is inconsistent or absent across the package
Audience Awareness Content reads as written for a specific age range (elementary or middle); language is approachable, encouraging, recipient-centered Generally accessible but occasionally slips into personal voice or overly mature vocabulary Content reads as written for the student, not the recipient
Handwritten Letter Quality Letter is legible, warm, 4–7 sentences, approved on first review; tone is specific and age-appropriate Letter approved after one revision; tone is solid but generic in places Letter required multiple revisions or did not reach approval standard
Reflection Completes the unit reflection form thoughtfully, connecting the service contribution to personal leadership growth Reflection completed but surface-level Reflection missing or superficial
📒 Grading efficiency tip

Drive folder names include the kiosk, and screenshot filenames typically include the student's name if they've enabled auto-naming on their Chromebook. Sort folders by name to grade one student's full package at a time without clicking through 90 files of unrelated work.

08

Student FAQ

Common questions students ask. Feel free to share this section directly or paraphrase in class.

How many kiosks do I have to complete?
All 6 pieces make up your full Smile Bag package: 5 kiosks (Puzzle It Out, Kindness Coupons, Calm-Down Toolkit, Dream Jar, Big Idea Planner) each take a screenshot + Drive upload, plus the Handwritten Letter which you hand directly to Ms. Marquand for approval.
Why do I need to pick a OneWord first? Can I just do the kiosks?
The OneWord is what ties your whole package together. A child opens the bag and sees one idea running through every piece — the riddle, the coupons, the breathing technique, the dream prompt, the planner, the letter. That's what makes it feel like a gift from a person, not a stack of printouts.
Do I have to use the same OneWord for all 6 pieces?
Yes. That's the point of the project — one word, six pieces, one connected gift.
Can I change my OneWord partway through?
You can, but it means redoing any kiosk that used the old word. Pick carefully at the start. If you're stuck, the Gemini Gem Partner is designed to help you find a word that fits you.
What if my screenshot is blurry or my upload doesn't show up in the tracker?
Most tracker gaps are Drive folder naming issues — if your upload doesn't show, check that you dropped it in the right child folder (the folder must match the kiosk name exactly). If the folder is right, wait 60 seconds and refresh. Screenshots should be readable but don't need to be perfect.
Why does the Handwritten Letter have to be approved before going in the bag?
Because it's going to a specific child. Ms. Marquand reads every letter before it leaves class — tone, legibility, signature, fit on the card. If it needs a rewrite, she'll tell you specifically what to fix. It's not about getting it "right," it's about making sure what reaches the child is something you'd be proud to sign.
Do I write my name on my kiosk cards?
On the letter, yes — sign with your first name. On the kiosk cards, usually no — the recipient gets a bag made by Leadership students as a group, not by a specific person. Ms. Marquand may direct you otherwise for specific activities.
Who receives these Smile Bags?
Elementary, middle, and high school students across the region going through medical procedures or recovery connected with Operation Smiles. The bags are a small way to let them know someone is thinking of them.
