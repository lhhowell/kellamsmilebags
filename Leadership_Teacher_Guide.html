Teacher Guide
Leadership Skills

Everything you need to deploy and run the 6-kiosk Smile Bags experience with your Leadership students — Drive setup, the live tracker, kiosk reference, the letter approval workflow, and the end-of-project reflection.

🌟 Katelyn Marquand Leadership Skills · Grades 9–12 6-Part Package · Per Student
🎯

What This Is. Leadership students build a complete 6-part Smile Bag package for a child recipient (elementary, middle, or high school). Five activities are made through interactive web kiosks. The sixth — the Handwritten Letter — is reviewed by you in person before being placed in the bag. A live tracker shows class-wide progress in real time.

Before Class Begins

One-Time Setup

Complete these steps once before launching the project. After this, the kiosks run themselves and the tracker updates automatically.

1
Confirm the Drive folder structure
A shared parent folder named Leadership Smile Bags · Spring 2026 contains six child folders with these exact names:
  • Kindness Coupons
  • Dream Jar
  • Calm-Down Toolkit
  • Puzzle It Out
  • Big Idea Planner
  • Handwritten Letter
Names must match exactly (spelling, spacing, hyphens). The tracker auto-discovers folders by name.
2
Verify the live tracker is connected
Open the Leadership Menu and look for the progress bar at the top. It should show counts for all 6 kiosks (starting at 0/90 each, totaling 0/540). If a tile shows —, the corresponding folder name in Drive doesn't match exactly. Fix the folder name and refresh.
3
Share the menu link with students
Post https://lhhowell.github.io/kellamsmilebags/Leadership_Menu.html on Canvas, in your Google Classroom stream, or project it during class. This is the students' single entry point — every kiosk and every Drive upload starts here.
4
Print or share the Reflection
The end-of-project Reflection is what students complete after they've built and submitted their bag's contents. Print copies or share the digital link — whichever fits your workflow. Details below in the Reflection section.

The Six Kiosks at a glance

Click any tile to open that kiosk in a new tab. Each kiosk follows the same flow: pick a OneWord → pick a category → pick an option → render a card students screenshot and upload to Drive.

Tab 01
🧩 Puzzle It Out
A puzzle for the recipient — riddle, brain teaser, or word game — with an optional reveal-the-answer button. 225 puzzles across 25 OneWords, scaled for grades 5–7.
Tab 02

A coupon redeemable for one kind action, sized for K–5 recipients. Three theme sets per OneWord: Creative, Active, and Observational.
Tab 03

Tab 05

A starter prompt + guiding questions for a 5×7 handwritten letter. Three age tracks: Elementary, Middle, High. Has the teacher-gate workflow — see below.
During Class

The Student Workflow

Every student follows the same loop for each of the 6 kiosks. They can do them in any order, across multiple class periods. Most students will reach 6 cards in 2–3 days of focused work.

1
Pick your OneWord
From the kiosk's tile of 25 OneWords, choose one that fits the child you're building for. The same word carries through all 6 kiosks — Smile Bag recipients receive a fully connected, themed package.
2
Pick a set or age
Each kiosk offers 3 themed sets (or 3 age tracks for the Letter). Pick the one that fits the recipient's age and personality.
3
Pick the specific card
3 options appear within the chosen set or age. Pick the one most giftable for this specific recipient. The kiosk renders the final card.
4
Screenshot the card
On a Chromebook, press Ctrl + Shift + Show Windows (or Print Screen on PC, Cmd + Shift + 4 on Mac). The card is the deliverable — saved as an image.
5
Upload to the right Drive folder
Click Drive Folder on the kiosk header to jump to the parent folder. Open the matching child folder. Drag the screenshot in. The tracker counts it within 60 seconds.
6
Repeat for the remaining kiosks
Same pattern, same OneWord. Each student produces 6 cards total — one per kiosk — all themed around their chosen OneWord.
Tab 06 · Special

The Letter Teacher Gate

The Handwritten Letter is the only kiosk with a teacher-review step. Students don't upload a screenshot — they hand you the physical 5×7 card before it goes in the bag.

1
Student picks an age track and prompt
The Letter Kiosk shows 3 age cards (Elementary, Middle, High) with a starter prompt and 2–3 guiding questions on each. Student picks the one that fits their recipient.
2
Student writes by hand on a 5×7 card
Using the prompt and guiding questions as a scaffold, the student handwrites the letter. Length: 4–7 sentences. Tone: warm, specific, positive. Voice: their own.
3
Student hands the card to you
Before the letter goes anywhere near the bag, you read it. The kiosk reminds them to check legibility, tone, signature, and that it fits the 5×7 space.
4
You approve or ask for a rewrite
If it passes, the card is placed in the bag. If something needs revision, hand it back with specific feedback — no formal "rejection" needed.
📋 Quality Check

When reading each letter, look for: (1) the OneWord is referenced or reflected in the message, (2) the tone fits the age group of the recipient, (3) nothing is crossed out or smudged, (4) the student signed with first name only (no last names for safety).

During the Project

The Live Tracker

The progress bar at the top of the menu page shows real-time class-wide progress. It updates automatically as students upload.

📊 What You'll See

A big number showing total uploads (goal: 540 — that's 90 students × 6 kiosks). Below it, six tiles — one per kiosk — showing per-kiosk progress out of 90. The numbers cache for 60 seconds, so the most recent upload may take a minute to appear. Click Refresh any time to force a fresh read.

⚡ Daily Use

Open the menu page on the projector at the start of each work session. Students see the tally live as their classmates upload. The visible progress is its own motivator — no grading needed.

⚠️ If a Tile Shows "—" or 0 Stays Stuck

The Drive folder name doesn't match what the tracker is looking for. Open the parent folder, check that the child folder is named exactly as listed in Step 1 above (case, spaces, and hyphens matter), and rename if needed. The tile will populate within 60 seconds.

After All Cards Are In

Bag Assembly

When a student has uploaded all 6 cards (5 kiosks + their approved letter), it's time to print and assemble.

Print each student's 5 screenshots from their Drive folders. Each is sized for a standard 4×6 or 5×7 print — adjust paper as needed.
Cut, fold, or trim cards as the format requires. The Kindness Coupons are designed to be cut apart along dashed lines.
Add the approved Handwritten Letter to the stack.
Place all 6 items in a Smile Bag along with any physical extras you've planned (snack, small toy, sticker, etc.).
Tag the bag with the recipient's age range so the right bag goes to the right child at the receiving end.
After the Project Ends

Student Reflection

After their bag is complete, every Leadership student completes a brief reflection. This is the moment the project turns inward — they've built for a stranger child, and now they pause to consider what that experience meant for them.

📝 What's Included

The Reflection prompts students to think about the OneWord they chose, the recipient they imagined, and what they noticed about themselves through the process. It can be completed digitally or printed. A separate file — Leadership_Reflection.html — handles this. See the Project Files section below for the link.

⏱️ Time

Plan for 15–25 minutes of focused class time. The Reflection isn't a quiz — there are no wrong answers — but it benefits from a quiet, undistracted environment.
