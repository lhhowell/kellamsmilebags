How it works
Dominant & Recessive Alleles
Every trait in your body is controlled by two alleles — one from each parent. A dominant allele (D) is expressed whenever it is present. A recessive allele (R) is only expressed when both alleles are recessive (RR). A Punnett square predicts the probability of each combination appearing in offspring.
DD
Homozygous Dominant
Two dominant alleles. Dominant trait is expressed.
DR
Heterozygous
One of each. Dominant trait is still expressed.
RR
Homozygous Recessive
Two recessive alleles. Recessive trait is expressed.
