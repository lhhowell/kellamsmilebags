Project 1 of 3

OneWord Stencil Set

Design a set of cardstock stencils — your word + 2 or 3 matching icons. A child will use them with crayons and colored pencils to create their own artwork.

AI.1 — Creative Thinking AI.2 — Creative Process AI.9 — Real-World Problem Solving AII.7 — Art for Community Needs AII.10 — Digital Tools
1
Choose Your OneWord
Select one of the 24 shared OneWords. This word will be the theme of everything you design. Think about which word speaks to you — and which icons feel right for it.
💡 Your word + icons need to feel connected. BLOOM → flower, butterfly, bee. ANCHOR → anchor, lighthouse, waves.
2
Research Your Icons
Look up the 2-3 icons assigned to your OneWord. Sketch simple, bold versions — stencils need thick outlines and no tiny details or they won't cut cleanly.
💡 Think silhouette first. If you can recognize it as a shadow, it works as a stencil.
3
Design Digitally
Using Canva, Adobe Express, or Illustrator, create your stencil designs:

• Word stencil — your OneWord in bold block letters, large enough to trace
• 2-3 icon stencils — simple, chunky shapes with thick outlines

Each stencil should fit on a 4×4 inch area of cardstock.
💡 Use thick strokes — minimum 8pt outline weight. Thin lines won't cut cleanly.
4
Submit Your File
Export your design as a PDF or SVG file and submit to your teacher. Your teacher will send the file to the Cricut for cutting.
💡 Name your file: YourLastName_OneWord_Stencil (e.g., Smith_Bloom_Stencil)
5
Cricut Cuts — You're Done!
The Cricut cuts your stencil set from cardstock. Your completed stencils go directly into a Smile Bag — along with a blank cardstock sheet, crayons, and colored pencils — for a child to use.
🎉 A child will use YOUR design to make their own art. That's your gift.
✦ What Goes in the Smile Bag
✂️ Your word stencil — cut from cardstock
✂️ 2–3 icon stencils — cut from cardstock
📄 One blank cardstock sheet
🖍️ Crayons (provided by project)
✏️ Colored pencils (provided by project)
Project 2 of 3

OneWord Jigsaw Puzzle

Design an original illustration inspired by your OneWord. It will be printed on cardstock and cut into puzzle pieces by the Cricut — a unique, handmade puzzle in every Smile Bag.

AI.1 — Creative Thinking AI.2 — Creative Process AI.16 — Contemporary Media AII.7 — Art for Community Needs AII.10 — Digital Tools AII.15 — Proficiency in Media
1
Choose Your OneWord
Select your OneWord. Your puzzle illustration should tell the story of that word — think about what scene, character, or idea represents it best for a child aged 5–12.
💡 Simple and bold works better than complex. This will be cut into pieces — strong shapes read better than fine detail.
2
Sketch Your Concept
Before going digital, sketch 2-3 thumbnail ideas. Think about:

• A central character or image (animal, plant, person, object)
• A simple background
• Your OneWord somewhere in the design
💡 Record your sketches in your sketchbook/journal — this is part of your creative process portfolio (AI.2h).
3
Create Your Digital Illustration
Using Canva, Adobe Express, Procreate, or Illustrator, create your puzzle illustration at exactly 5×7 inches, 300 DPI.

• Use bold colors and strong contrast
• Include your OneWord in the design
• Keep details simple — this will be cut into 9–12 pieces
💡 Imagine the puzzle assembled — does it look complete and beautiful? That's your goal.
4
Submit Your File
Export as PDF or high-resolution PNG and submit to your teacher.
💡 Name your file: YourLastName_OneWord_Puzzle (e.g., Jones_Transform_Puzzle)
5
Print · Cut · Into the Bag!
Your design is printed on cardstock and the Cricut cuts it into puzzle pieces. A child will assemble YOUR illustration — then color it, display it, or keep it.
🎉 Every piece of that puzzle was designed by you.
✦ What Goes in the Smile Bag
🧩 Your original illustration — printed on cardstock, cut into 9–12 puzzle pieces
📦 Pieces bundled together in a small envelope or bag
Project 3 of 3

Smile Mirror Design

Design a smile-themed illustration that will be printed and adhered to the back of a 3D-printed acrylic mirror — the signature item in every Smile Bag.

AI.1 — Creative Thinking AI.2 — Creative Process AII.1 — Personal Style AII.7 — Art for Community Needs AII.10 — Digital Tools AII.15 — Proficiency in Media
1
Understand the Canvas
The mirror backing is a circle — 3 inches in diameter. Your design must work in a circular format. Think about how to compose an image that fills and completes a round shape.
💡 Circular format is a real professional design constraint. Mandalas, medallions, badges, coins, seals — study how artists fill circles.
2
Design Your Smile Image
Your design should feature a smile — joyful, warm, and child-friendly. It can include:

• A smiling face, sun, animal, or character
• Your OneWord or a short message
• Decorative elements around the circular border
💡 Think about who will look at this. A child receiving a Smile Bag sees this mirror every time they look at their reflection. Make it feel like a hug.
3
Create Your Digital Design
Using Canva, Adobe Express, or Illustrator, create your design at exactly 3×3 inches, 300 DPI, circular crop.

• Bold colors that stand out
• Clear central image
• Text (if any) must be large enough to read at 3 inches
💡 In Canva: use a circle template or create a 3×3 document and add a circle mask. Design inside the circle only.
4
Submit Your File
Export as PDF or PNG with transparent background and submit to your teacher.
💡 Name your file: YourLastName_OneWord_Mirror (e.g., Davis_Shine_Mirror)
5
Print · Trim · Adhere · Into the Bag!
Your design is printed, trimmed to circle, and adhered to the back of the 3D-printed smile disc mirror. Every child who looks in that mirror sees your art first.
🎉 Your design is on a mirror that a child will keep and use. That's a real art commission.
✦ What Goes in the Smile Bag
🪞 3D-printed smile disc mirror (from AP Environmental Science)
🎨 Your circular design — printed and adhered to the mirror back
