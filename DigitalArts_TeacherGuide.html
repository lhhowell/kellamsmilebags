Three Projects

What Students Create

✂️ OneWord Stencil Set

Students design their OneWord in bold letters + 2-3 matching icons. Cricut cuts the stencil set from cardstock. Goes in the bag with blank cardstock, crayons, and colored pencils.

4×4 inch each stencil PDF or SVG export Cardstock weight: 65–80 lb

🧩 Jigsaw Puzzle

Students create an original illustration inspired by their OneWord. Printed on cardstock and Cricut-cut into 9-12 puzzle pieces. Child assembles and colors it.

5×7 inches 300 DPI PDF or PNG export 9–12 pieces

🪞 Mirror Design

Students design a smile-themed circular illustration. Printed and adhered to the back of the 3D-printed acrylic mirror disc (from AP Environmental Science).

3 inch diameter circle 300 DPI PNG with transparent bg
Implementation

Class Workflow

📋 Before the Activity
  • Share the Student Guide link with students via Google Classroom or display on screen: lhhowell.github.io/kellamsmilebags/DigitalArts_StudentGuide.html
  • Confirm Cricut is loaded with cardstock (65-80 lb weight recommended)
  • Ensure students have access to their preferred design tool — Canva, Adobe Express, or Illustrator
  • Students choose their OneWord — no duplicates within a class if possible
  • Establish a shared Google Drive folder for file submissions, organized by project type
🖥️ Student Design Phase (1–2 class periods)
  • Students follow the Student Guide step-by-step for their assigned project
  • Encourage sketchbook planning first before going digital — this aligns to AI.2 (creative process portfolio)
  • Circulate and check that designs have thick outlines and bold shapes — thin details won't cut cleanly on Cricut
  • For stencils: remind students to check that letter shapes are connected — open counters (the hole in O, A, B) need bridges or they fall out
  • For puzzle: check that designs are 5×7 inches at 300 DPI before export
  • For mirror: check that designs are circular and 3 inches at 300 DPI
📁 File Submission
Naming convention:
  • Stencil: LastName_Word_Stencil.pdf
  • Puzzle: LastName_Word_Puzzle.pdf or .png
  • Mirror: LastName_Word_Mirror.png

Students submit to the shared Google Drive folder. You batch-send to Cricut for cutting.
✂️ Cricut Cutting
  • Stencils: Import SVG or PDF into Cricut Design Space. Use "Cut" setting. Cardstock 65-80 lb. Each student's set = word + 2-3 icons on one sheet
  • Puzzle: Print first, then cut with Cricut using a jigsaw puzzle cut template in Design Space. Score lines before cutting
  • Mirror: Print and trim with Cricut using circle cut — 3 inch diameter. Adhere to mirror back with double-sided tape
