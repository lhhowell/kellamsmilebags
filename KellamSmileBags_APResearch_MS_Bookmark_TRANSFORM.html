TRANSFORM
Becoming something completely different
🔬 Did You Know?
A caterpillar doesn't just grow wings — it dissolves nearly all of its cells into liquid, then rebuilds itself using clusters called imaginal discs. In 21 days, its DNA rewrites an entire body plan. Scientists are studying this process to understand cellular reprogramming in human medicine.

Try It! Activity
What You Need
🔍 Your magnifying glass
📓 Your observation journal card
(both are in this bag!)
Try It!
1
Look at the back of your hand through the magnifying glass. Count the tiny lines. Those are the same skin cells that have been transforming — dividing and rebuilding — your entire life.
2
On your journal card, draw something that transformed this year — a skill, a mindset, a relationship. What did it look like before? What does it look like now?
3
Here's the real question scientists are asking: if a caterpillar can completely rewrite its body — what can you rewrite? Write one word as your answer.
A Message For You
"The more I studied transformation at the cellular level, the more I understood — change is not something that happens to you. It is something your cells have been doing for you, quietly, your whole life. You are already built to transform."
— Jordan, Your Kellam Scientist · Kellam High School
