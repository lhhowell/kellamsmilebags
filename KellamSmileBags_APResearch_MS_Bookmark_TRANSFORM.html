🔬 Did You Know?
Inside a chrysalis, a caterpillar doesn’t just grow wings — it completely dissolves into a liquid soup of cells, then reorganizes into an entirely new creature. Scientists call this histolysis and histogenesis. The same organism, transformed at the cellular level.

Try It! — Science Activity
Middle School Edition
What You Need
🌞 Your crayons or colored pencils
📓 Your mini notepad
(both are in this bag!)
🧪 Your Hypothesis First
Before you start: if you mixed all your crayon colors together, what color do you predict you would get? Write it down — then test it!
Try It!
1
Layer 3 crayon colors on top of each other on paper. Watch how they transform into a new color — this is color mixing, the same science as light and pigment.
2
In your notepad, record: what colors did you mix? What color appeared? Was your hypothesis right?
3
What if… you added water? Or pressure? Science always asks — what happens NEXT when conditions change?
