📚 The 5-Step Flow
1
Open the Science Supply Bar
From the AP Research Menu, click Science Supply Bar. Read each supply card and consider how it connects to your research thinking.
2
Pick your OneWord and 1–3 supplies
Choose a OneWord that captures the spirit of your research. Then tap 1–3 supplies that connect to your word and your reasoning. Your selections log automatically. Click Confirm My Supplies.
3
Launch the Bookmark Builder
Click Create My Bookmark. The Bookmark Builder opens with your OneWord and supplies ready. Add your name, write a quick fact about your supplies, list 3 simple steps for the elementary student to try, and add a personal message.
4
Save and Print
Click Save My Bookmark — your work logs to the spreadsheet. Then click Print Bookmark. The Front and Back print side-by-side as a single landscape page with a dashed fold line.
5
Cut, Fold, Glue
Cut once around the outside of the printed pair. Fold forward along the dashed center line so the Front faces out and the Back faces out (like a greeting card). Glue the two inside surfaces together. Laminate if you have time. Place in your Smile Bag.
⚡ Why “honest” matters Choose supplies that genuinely connect to your OneWord and your research. A student picking seeds for the word GROW because they study germination is stronger than a student picking stickers because they're nearby. Let your scientific reasoning guide you — then explain it on the bookmark.
🔖 Worked Example
OneWord: CONNECT · Supplies: Play-Doh + Stickers
CONNECT
Building Bridges Together
📚 DID YOU KNOW?
Two things connect when they share a piece of themselves.
CONNECT
Try It! Activity
🌿 What You Need
Play-Doh (any 2 colors) and a few stickers.
⚡ Try It!
  1. 1Roll two Play-Doh balls in different colors. Press a sticker onto each one.
  2. 2Smush the two balls together. Notice how the colors connect at the seam.
  3. 3Pull them apart slowly. What stays? What goes? That’s how connections work.
💙 A Message For You
You connect to people every day — family, friends, even strangers who smile back. Each connection makes you a little stronger. Keep building yours!
— Your AP Research Scientist
