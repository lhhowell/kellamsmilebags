Learning Objective
Students will use a browser-based digital paint canvas to create an original coloring sheet design inspired by their OneWord. Students will make intentional color and design choices that reflect the word's meaning, download and print their completed artwork, and contribute it to a Smile Bag as a gift for an elementary student to color. The lesson integrates creative expression, digital tool use, and reflective thinking about how visual art communicates meaning.

90-Minute Lesson Timeline
Block Schedule
0:00
10 min
Introduction — Art as Care
Teacher opens with examples of how food presentation and plating are forms of visual art — and how a beautifully designed coloring sheet can give a child something to look forward to. Brief gallery walk of OneWord coloring examples from the Smile Bags collection projected on screen.
0:10
10 min
Kiosk Demo & Tool Exploration
Teacher demonstrates the Bake the Code digital paint canvas on the projector — showing brush tools, color palette, eraser, fill tool, undo, and download. Students spend 5 minutes freely exploring the tool before beginning their design.
0:20
10 min
Design Planning
Students sketch a quick thumbnail of their planned design on paper before beginning digitally. Teacher prompts: What does your word look like as an image? What colors feel right for this word? What would make a K–5 student want to color this?
0:30
45 min
Digital Paint Studio
Students work independently to create their coloring sheet design using the digital paint canvas. Teacher circulates to support creative decisions and technical tool use. Mid-point check at 20 minutes — teacher provides brief feedback to each student.
  • Intro to Culinary: focus on completing a clean, clear design with at least 3 distinct elements
  • Catering: focus on composition quality, visual balance, and intentional color choices
1:15
10 min
Download, Print & Reflect
Students download their finished design and print on cardstock. Printed sheets are packaged for Smile Bags. Students write a 2-sentence reflection: what they designed and why they think an elementary student will enjoy it. Reflections may be tucked into the bag alongside the coloring sheet.
📊
Assessment
🎨 Design Completion
Student completes a full coloring sheet design with at least 3 distinct visual elements and intentional color or composition choices.
🔗 Word Connection
Design visually reflects the meaning of the student's OneWord — identifiable connection between the image and the word's concept.
🖨️ Print Quality
Downloaded and printed image is clean, correctly sized, and suitable for inclusion in a Smile Bag — appropriate for a K–5 audience.
✍️ Reflection
Two-sentence written reflection demonstrating intentional design thinking and empathy for the elementary student who will receive the coloring sheet.
🌱
Differentiation
🤝 Support
Provide a pre-drawn outline the student can color digitally rather than draw from scratch. Word meaning card available for reference. Allow verbal reflection in place of written.
✅ On Level
Student independently creates an original design, downloads and prints it, and completes a written reflection connecting design to word meaning.
🚀 Extension
Student creates two versions — one B&W coloring sheet and one fully colored reference — and writes a designer's note explaining choices. Presents to class before packaging.
💙
Smile Bags Connection
The coloring sheet created in this lesson goes directly into a Smile Bag for an elementary student. For many children, a coloring page is a moment of calm in an otherwise difficult day. Every line a Culinary Arts student draws is an act of care for a child they will never meet — and that is exactly what Word & Wonder is about.
