At Kellam High School in Virginia Beach, Digital Arts students are creating original AI-generated art, hand-designed stencils, laminated jigsaw puzzles, and original adhesive stickers for the Smile Mirror — each one connected to a shared OneWord and placed in a Smile Bag for a child in the community. Every piece of art was made by a student who didn't know the child's name, but made something just for them.

At the heart of Smile Bags are 25 OneWords — connecting environmental science to social emotional well-being.

Each of the 25 OneWords lives in two worlds at once — in the natural systems students study, and in the inner lives students navigate every day. From Absorb to Transform, these words remind us that the same forces that sustain ecosystems are the forces that sustain people too.

Who Receives a Smile Bag

A bag finds its way to a student
who needs it most.

Smile Bags are distributed across Kellam's eight feeder schools and Kellam High School itself — ten bags per school, ninety students reached. Guidance counselors identify students who may need encouragement, motivation, or simply a reason to smile. A bag is chosen for them deliberately, not randomly — at exactly the right moment.

Every item inside was made by a Kellam student who didn't know your name — but made something just for you.

Physical Deliverable · Contribution 03
Jigsaw Puzzle
Students prepare a Set G nature poster image as a jigsaw puzzle — printed, laminated, and cut into 8–20 pieces. Piece count must match the recipient's age and ability. A 20-piece puzzle designed for a 6-year-old is a design failure regardless of how well it was cut. This contribution pairs technical production skills with recipient awareness.
