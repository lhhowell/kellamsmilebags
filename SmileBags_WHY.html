🌍 The Project

Students building Smile Bags

At our high school in Virginia Beach, Virginia, students are building Smile Bags — bags of small, intentionally designed items that travel into the hands of someone who needs a smile.

Every item in every bag is student-made. Not bought. Not ordered. Made. By you, by your classmates, by students across our school connected through one shared word.

“Technology becomes a pathway for empathy, connection, and positive social impact.”
💙 The Bigger Mission

A student-driven service initiative

This is service-learning — spreading kindness and meeting community needs in meaningful ways. The work is small in scale and large in intention. Every bag is a quiet act, but the project as a whole reaches further than any single one of us could on our own.

You’re not just completing an assignment. You’re contributing to something that connects classes across our school, brings teachers and students together around a shared purpose, and lands gently in the day of someone you’ll likely never meet.

🌿 The 25 OneWords

Words that live in two worlds at once

At the heart of Smile Bags are 25 OneWords — a shared set of words that connect environmental science to social emotional well-being.

Each word was chosen because it lives in two worlds at the same time: in the natural systems we study, and in the inner lives we navigate every day. From Absorb to Transform, these words remind us that the same forces sustaining ecosystems — balance, flow, renewal, resilience — are the forces that sustain people too.

In Ecosystems
Balance · Flow · Renewal · Adaptation · Resilience · Cycles
In People
Balance · Flow · Renewal · Adaptation · Resilience · Cycles
⚡ Why Your Work Matters

A small thing, given with care

The recipient of your Smile Bag will not know your name. They’ll receive something handmade by a student they’ll never meet, built around a word that connects environmental science to the human experience.

That’s the whole point. The bag is the message. The word is the bridge. The care you put into making it is the real gift.

You don’t need to make something perfect. You need to make something honest — an item that reflects real thought, real choice, and real connection between your OneWord, your supplies, and a person you’ll never see.
