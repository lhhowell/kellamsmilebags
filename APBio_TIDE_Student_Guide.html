Somewhere, an elementary student is going to open your bag.

Smile Bags go to Three Oaks Elementary students in grades K–5. Guidance counselors identify students who may need encouragement, motivation, or simply a reason to smile. A bag is chosen for them deliberately — not randomly — at exactly the right moment.

You will never meet the child who receives your bag. But every item you create is an act of care for someone real. That's what makes this different from a class project. This one matters beyond the grade.

Your Zoo Project → Your Bag

From the Field to the Bag

Your AP Biology Zoo Project doesn't just live in a presentation. Use what you observed and created at the Norfolk Zoo to build something real for an elementary student. Here's how each project type translates:

Zoo Option 1
Exhibit Design
↓ becomes
An animal fact card or 3D printed model of the animal, designed for a K–5 student to hold and learn from.
Fact card · 3D model
✨ Example
A 5×7 card titled “Meet the African Lion” with a hand-illustrated front, 3 kid-friendly facts about lion behavior, and a trophic cascade diagram on the back — plus a 3D printed lion figurine tucked in the bag beside it.
Zoo Option 2
Behavioral Observation Journal
↓ becomes
A behavior scene puzzle or activity card the elementary student can complete themselves.
Puzzle · Activity card
✨ Example
A 4-panel scrambled story card showing a sea otter’s morning routine — the child cuts out the panels and puts them in the right order, learning about otter behavior while they play.
Zoo Option 3
Comparative Anatomy
↓ becomes
A coloring sheet with labeled anatomy — simplified for a young student, beautifully designed.
Coloring sheet
✨ Example
A beautifully illustrated coloring sheet of a frog with 5 labeled body parts — eyes, webbed feet, tympanum, sticky tongue, and permeable skin — each with a one-sentence kid-friendly explanation in the margin.
Zoo Option 4
Conservation Campaign
↓ becomes
A fold-out conservation card with a kid-friendly message and one simple action step.
Fold-out card · Brochure
✨ Example
A tri-fold card titled “Save the Sea Turtle” — outside shows a bold illustration, inside shows 3 threats sea turtles face and 1 simple action step a child can take today, like picking up litter at the beach.
Zoo Option 5
Food Web / Ecosystem Model
↓ becomes
A food web puzzle or drag-and-drop activity card the elementary student builds themselves.
Puzzle · Activity card
✨ Example
A 4-panel scrambled story card showing a sea otter’s morning routine — the child cuts out the panels and puts them in the right order, learning about otter behavior while they play.
Zoo Option 6
Student-Designed Project
↓ becomes
An illustrated children's book page, an educational activity, or a creative original piece.
Children's book · Original

Together, your class and T.I.D.E. Coalition Club are filling 10 bags for elementary students. Browse the Creation Kiosk and contribute activities that connect to your Zoo Project, class and club content, and your OneWord.

All items must be digitally designed and printed or produced unless otherwise noted. Print size is 5×7 unless specified.

📌 All supplies and materials listed in the project are provided. Any materials not listed are the student's responsibility to obtain or must be approved by your sponsor.

Sponsor & Project Information

This project is sponsored by Elena O'Brien for both AP Biology and the T.I.D.E. Coalition. AP Biology students connect this project to their Zoo Project capstone. T.I.D.E. Coalition members participate as a club service project.

For 3D printed items, email your shareable Tinkercad link to lhhowell@vbschools.com for approval before printing. Your design will be printed in D210 once approved.

Questions? Reach out to Ms. O'Brien before you begin.
