Somewhere, an elementary student is going to open your bag.

Smile Bags go to North Landing Elementary students in grades K–5. Guidance counselors identify students who may need encouragement, motivation, or simply a reason to smile. A bag is chosen for them deliberately — not randomly — at exactly the right moment.

You will never meet the child who receives your bag. But every item you create is an act of care for someone real. That's what makes this different from a class project. This one matters beyond the grade.

Choose 10 items total spanning at least 3 categories from the Creation Kiosk. Your Zoo Project deliverable should inspire at least 1–2 of your items. All items must be digitally designed and printed or produced unless otherwise noted. Print size is 5×7 unless specified.

📌 All supplies and materials listed in the project are provided. Any materials not listed are the student's responsibility to obtain or must be approved by your sponsor.
Your Bag

Core Items — Complete All 5

These 5 items are required for every bag. They ensure every elementary student receives a complete, intentional experience.

1
Zoo Project Bag Item
Translate your AP Biology Zoo Project into one physical item for the bag. Use the chart above to match your project type to a bag item format. This is your science contribution — designed to be accessible and engaging for a K–5 student.
Required · Zoo Project5×7 or appropriate format
2
Elementary Science Supply Bar + Bookmark
Visit the Elementary Science Supply Bar and choose 1 or 2 supplies. Use the Bookmark Builder Kiosk to design 1 bookmark connecting your supply to your OneWord through language a K–5 student can understand. Your bookmark + supplies go into the bag together.
Required · CoreBookmark + 1–2 supplies
3
Coding or Decoding Activity
Design an original coding or decoding activity for the elementary student. This could be a binary coding sheet, a secret cipher, or a color-by-code activity where solving the puzzle reveals an animal, ecosystem scene, or your OneWord. Must be kid-friendly and completable with crayons or a pencil.
Required · Core5×7 · B&W or color
4
Wellness or Belonging Item
Design an item focused on calm, encouragement, belonging, or joy — reflecting the T.I.D.E. Coalition's values. This could be an affirmation card set, a breathing exercise card, a calm corner guide, or a creative wellness activity. Designed with a K–5 student in mind.
Required · T.I.D.E.5×7
5
Handwritten Letter ✍️
Write a warm, personal letter to the elementary student who will receive your bag. Keep it encouraging and age-appropriate. Written on a 5×7 card or quality paper. Sign with your first name only. This is the only handwritten item permitted in your bag.
Handwritten · One Item Only5×7 · First name only
Part Two

Additional Items — Choose 5 from the Creation Kiosk

Your remaining 5 items come from the AP Bio & TIDE Creation Kiosk Menu. Browse all four categories and choose any 5 items that aren't already covered in your core items above. Aim for variety — your bag should feel like a thoughtful collection that an elementary student will genuinely enjoy. Think about what would make a K–5 student's day.
