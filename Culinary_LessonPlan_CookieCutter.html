Cookie Cutter Design & Order
Students design and order 3D-printed cookie cutters, then use them to produce cookies for Smile Bags.

Learning Objective
Students will design and order a custom 3D-printed cookie cutter using a web-based kiosk, selecting their OneWord shape, school level, and filament color. In the second half of the lesson, students will use cookie cutters to produce decorated cookies that will be packaged into Smile Bags for elementary students — combining digital design thinking with hands-on culinary production.
📋
Future Ready Virginia Beach Standards
FR · CTE
Career & Technical Education: Students apply professional baking and production techniques to create a finished food product intended for community distribution — modeling industry-standard quality and consistency.
FR · IT

FR · SR

FR · DL

Materials & Resources

  • Bambu Lab 3D Printer (D210 — ITS Lab)
  • Google Sheets order queue (auto-populated)
Culinary
  • Sugar cookie dough (pre-made or from-scratch)

90-Minute Lesson Timeline
Block Schedule
0:00
10 min
Introduction — 3D Printing & Design Thinking
Teacher introduces the 3D printing process and shows examples of completed cookie cutters. Students see how their digital order becomes a physical tool. Brief overview of the kiosk and how orders flow to the print queue in D210.
0:10
20 min
Kiosk — Design & Submit Order
Students open the Cookie Cutter Order Kiosk and configure their order: OneWord selection, school level (elementary/middle/high school shape style), filament color from 7 options, and quantity. Order logs to the print queue automatically.
  • Elementary shapes: image-based (animal, nature, object)
  • High School shapes: word-text shaped cutter
  • Students choose color based on their word's meaning
0:30
10 min
Mise en Place & Lab Prep
Students transition to the lab, wash hands, and set up their stations. Cookie dough is distributed or prepared. Students review decorating plan and identify which cutter they will use from available printed inventory.
0:40
35 min
Cookie Production Lab
Students roll, cut, and bake cookies using their OneWord cookie cutters. Teacher focuses on consistency of thickness, proper baking temperature, and professional presentation expectations.
  • Intro to Culinary: focus on technique fundamentals — rolling, cutting, transfer to sheet
  • Catering: focus on production volume, consistency, and packaging standards
1:15
10 min
Quality Check, Packaging & Clean Up
Cooled cookies are inspected for quality, packaged for Smile Bags, and labeled. Stations are cleaned and sanitized to industry standards. Teacher debriefs: What did the 3D printing process add to the experience? How does the cookie shape connect to the word?
📊
Assessment
🖥️ Order Submission
Completed and submitted cookie cutter order logged to the print queue with all required fields: word, shape, color, quantity.
🍪 Product Quality
Teacher evaluation of baked cookies — consistent thickness, proper bake, clean cut shape, professional appearance suitable for Smile Bag packaging.
📦 Packaging
Cookies correctly packaged and labeled per Smile Bags standards — food-safe wrapping, word label visible, bag-ready presentation.
🧹 Station Sanitation
End-of-lab station check — all surfaces cleaned, tools washed and stored, waste properly disposed of.
🌱
Differentiation
🤝 Support
Teacher assists with kiosk navigation. Pre-rolled dough option available. Student paired with a lab partner for production. Fewer cookies required for packaging quota.
✅ On Level
Student independently submits order, prepares and bakes cookies to standard, and packages product for Smile Bags.
🚀 Extension
Student designs a custom icing decoration that reflects the word's meaning and writes a brief card explaining the design choice to be included with the cookies.
💙
Smile Bags Connection
The cookies produced in this lesson go directly into Smile Bags destined for Three Oaks Elementary students. The cookie cutter shape — custom-designed and 3D-printed specifically for this project — makes every cookie a one-of-a-kind gift. Students are not just baking cookies. They are making something no child has ever received before.
