How your word becomes a song — and why it always sounds amazing

Start Here Words You Need to Know
🎼 Note
A single musical sound with its own pitch — like one key on a piano. Think of it as one "step" in a melody.
🎹 Scale
A group of notes that sound good together, arranged from low to high. Like a musical "team" where everyone gets along.
🎸 Pentatonic
"Penta" = 5, "tonic" = notes. A scale with only 5 notes — the most naturally pleasing sounds the human ear knows!
🎵 Pitch
How high or low a note sounds. A bird chirping = high pitch. A foghorn = low pitch.
🎶 Melody
A sequence of notes played one after another — what you hum or whistle. Your word's DNA becomes a mini-melody!

The Secret Weapon Why It Always Sounds Good

Here's the wildest thing about Compose It! — it is mathematically impossible to make your word sound bad. That's not luck. That's science! 🔬

Every note in your word is chosen from the pentatonic scale — a 5-note scale that humans have loved for thousands of years. Ancient Greek musicians used it. African drums use it. The Beatles used it. It's in country music, jazz, rock, and pop.

🤯
Mind-blowing fact: The pentatonic scale removes the two notes that normally clash with each other. What's left? Five notes that harmonize with anything. Scientists believe we're actually wired at birth to find this scale pleasing — it may be hardcoded into human biology!
