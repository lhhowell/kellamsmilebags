At Kellam High School in Virginia Beach, AVID students are crafting original inspirational quotes for children who need encouragement. Every quote is built around one shared word — chosen with intention, written with purpose, and printed on cardstock for a real child's Smile Bag.

At the heart of Smile Bags are 25 OneWords — connecting environmental science to social emotional well-being.

Each of the 25 OneWords lives in two worlds at once — in the natural systems students study, and in the inner lives students navigate every day. From Absorb to Transform, these words remind us that the same forces that sustain ecosystems — balance, flow, renewal, resilience — are the forces that sustain people too.

Who Receives a Smile Bag

A bag finds its way to a student
who needs it most.

Smile Bags are distributed across Kellam's eight feeder schools and Kellam High School itself — ten bags per school, ninety students reached. Guidance counselors identify students who may need encouragement, motivation, or simply a reason to smile. A bag is chosen for them deliberately, not randomly — at exactly the right moment.

Every item inside was made by a Kellam student who didn't know your name — but made something just for you.

How It Works
Student Workflow
  • 1
    Choose a Card Shape
    Square (4″ × 4″) or Circle (4″ round, Cricut-cut). The shape sets the canvas for their card.
  • 2
    Select a OneWord
    Choose one of the 25 shared OneWords. Each word displays its meaning to help students connect deeply.
  • 3
    Costa's Quick Write Reflection
    Students respond to a Level 3 prompt — applying their word as wisdom for a child who needs it. This reflection fuels the quote.
  • 4
    Gemini AI Writing Tutor
    Students open the Gemini Gem tutor, share their reflection, and receive Socratic questioning to deepen and distill their thinking.
  • 5
    Write the Quote (120 characters)
    Final original inspirational quote — refined through the tutor conversation and entered into the kiosk.
  • 6
    Choose Color Pair & Font
    Select from 7 school color pairs and 4 font styles. The kiosk shows a live preview of the finished card.
  • 7
    Preview, Submit & Print
    Students review their card design and submit. Ms. Vinson prints on cardstock and cuts to final size.
