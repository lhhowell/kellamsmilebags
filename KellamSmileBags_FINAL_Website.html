Smile Bags.

A cross-curricular service-learning project connecting Kellam students to elementary, middle, and high school students in our community who need a smile.

Each smile bag contains items handmade by Kellam students — one item per class, one word connecting them all. The child who receives a BLOOM bookmark also receives the BLOOM coloring book page, written for them by a Kellam student. The 25 OneWord vocabulary words were selected because each carries two meanings — a science meaning rooted in VBCPS Environmental Science standards, and a human meaning grounded in social-emotional awareness, written directly for a child who is healing.

Participating Classes

Who's Building the Bags

Each participating class and club contributes at least one student-made item to every smile bag — some contribute more. Document packages will be available for viewing after the project is completed in Spring 2026.

Smile Bag Guidelines

All smile bag items must be student-made. The only acceptable purchased items are crayons, colored pencils, paint kits, and Play-Doh — these may be included to accompany the student-made coloring book page.

✦ New items will be added as we bring on more participants.

● AP Research

Did You Know? Research Bookmark

Abbie McGee · AP Research course framework · Science Supply Bar · Bookmark Builder Kiosk

AP Research students translate a year of research thinking into a 'Did You Know?' bookmark for a student in our community. Each student selects their OneWord, browses the Science Supply Bar — choosing 1–3 hands-on supplies based on their own scientific reasoning — and builds their bookmark using the digital Bookmark Builder Kiosk. The bookmark features a research fact, a student-designed activity using their chosen supplies, and a personal message signed Your Kellam Scientist. This is inquiry-based, differentiated, and authentic — every bookmark is genuinely different because every scientist thinks differently. Future Ready attributes: Thinkers & Inquirers — selecting tools based on scientific hypothesis and reasoning; Personally & Socially Responsible — applying research expertise in direct service of a student who needs a smile.

🔬 Contribution: Research bookmark + science supplies · printed on cardstock · laminated

● Physics

Spinning Top Design

Student guidelines · Virginia SOL PH.3 / PH.4 · Tinkercad + Bambu Lab

Students design an original spinning top in Tinkercad, applying physics concepts including rotational inertia, angular momentum, and center of mass to their design decisions — directly connecting Virginia SOL PH.3 (forces and Newton's laws) and PH.4 (conservation laws including momentum). ● Introduction to Engineering (8540)

3D Printed Assembly Kit

Student guidelines · Virginia CTE Introduction to Engineering (8540) · WRS 1 / WRS 5 / WRS 14 · OnShape CAD + Bambu Lab

Introduction to Engineering (8540) students design a 5–8 piece snap-fit assembly kit in Tinkercad — no glue, no tools, pieces click together. Students choose from four model categories: simple vehicles (car, boat, airplane, helicopter), mechanical items (gear set, windmill, lever, pulley), or nature-inspired models (butterfly, turtle, fish, gecko). An optional OneWord connection is encouraged but not required. Printed in orange PLA on the Bambu Lab printer. Future Ready attributes: Problem Solvers & Value Creators — applying engineering design thinking to fabricate a functional multi-part object; Thinkers & Inquirers — investigating snap-fit tolerances and design iteration.

🔧 Contribution: 3D printed snap-fit assembly kit (5–8 pieces)

● Advanced Entrepreneurship

OneWord Sticker Design

Student guidelines · Virginia CTE Entrepreneurship · WRS 1 / WRS 5 / WRS 14 · Canva · Adobe Express · Google Slides · Cricut Design Space

Advanced Entrepreneurship students apply design thinking and entrepreneurial principles to create a OneWord sticker for a student in our community. Each student selects one of the 25 shared OneWords and designs a sticker in their tool of choice — Canva, Adobe Express, Google Slides, or Cricut Design Space. Designs are uploaded to Cricut Design Space and printed on adhesive sticker paper in two size options: 2.0 inch round or 2.5 inch square. Students connect branding, customer focus, and value creation to a real product with a real purpose. Future Ready attributes: Problem Solvers & Value Creators — designing a product that serves a real customer need; Personally & Socially Responsible — putting entrepreneurial skills to work for the greater good.

🏷️ Contribution: OneWord sticker — 2.0 inch round or 2.5 inch square · printed on adhesive sticker paper
