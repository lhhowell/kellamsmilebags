How to Write a Smile Bag Card

A short guide to writing a handwritten note that actually lands for a middle schooler.
AP Environmental Science · Honaker · Spring 2026
01

Who is this card for?

Your card is going to a middle schooler — 6th, 7th, or 8th grade — who could use a smile today. They might be nervous, tired, recovering from a medical procedure, or just having a rough week.

Your words matter, even the short ones. One honest sentence from a real person is worth more than a whole page of cliches.

02

Seven tips for a great note

You have a 4×4" space on the Smile Bags letterhead. Two to four lines is perfect — then fill the rest with doodles, color, and personality.

1

Keep it short.

Two to four lines. The blank space on the card is part of the gift — it means you wrote with intention.

2

Sound like a person, not a poster.

Middle schoolers can spot a cheesy fake-inspirational message instantly. Write it the way you would say it to a friend.

3

Acknowledge that middle school is real.

They are dealing with big feelings, hard days, and a lot of change. A card that says "I see you" lands harder than "Have a great day!"

4

Be specific when you can.

"You're awesome" is kind. "The way you keep trying when stuff is hard is awesome" is better.

5

Sign your first name only.

Keeps it personal and keeps it safe. No last names, no school, no contact info.

6

Keep it positive.

No sad, dark, or political content. Every card is a gift. If you wouldn't want to receive it, don't send it.

7

Make it yours — embellish.

Doodle a small illustration, add a border, draw a flower next to your OneWord, use colored pens, add a small sticker. A handmade card feels like a gift, not a worksheet — and the decoration is often what the recipient remembers first.

03

Before you turn it in

Quick pre-flight check

  • Read it out loud. Does it sound like you?
  • Check spelling and punctuation.
  • Ask yourself: would a 12-year-old be happy to open this?
  • First name signed? No last name, school, or contact info?
  • Writing stays inside the 4×4 space (below the heart divider)?
  • Added color, doodles, or small decoration to make it feel handmade?
04

Three examples — three different voices

You don't have to copy these. Pick the voice closest to how you actually write, and make yours.

Short & real
Hey friend —
Middle school is hard.
So are you.
Keep showing up.
— Maya
OneWord anchored
BLOOM.You don't have to rush or be like anyone else. Take your time. You're growing exactly right.
— Jordan
Warm & honest
Reminder:

— Daniela

About the letterhead

Write on the Smile Bags 4×4 letterhead Ms. Honaker will provide. The dashed lines on the full sheet show where to cut — one printed page gives you four cards. Write below the heart divider so the mascots stay visible at the top of your message — then use the space around your words to doodle, color, and make it unmistakably yours.
