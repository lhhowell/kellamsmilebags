💻 The Four Kiosk Options
All four options are accessed from the Culinary Arts Kiosk hub. Direct students to: lhhowell.github.io/kellamsmilebags/Culinary_Menu.html
Option A
🖨️ Cookie Cutter Order
Culinary_Kiosk_v3.html
  • Student enters name + last initial
  • Chooses recipient level (Elementary, Middle, or High School)
  • Selects their OneWord → cutter shape appears
  • Picks filament color from Bambu palette
  • Submits → logs to Cookie Cutter Orders spreadsheet
Option B
📖 OneWord Recipe Card
Culinary_RecipeCard.html
  • Student chooses their OneWord
  • Picks Simple (family-friendly) or Advanced (culinary-level) recipe
  • Both recipes are connected to the word's meaning
  • Adds optional personal message
  • Prints card → goes in the Smile Bag
  • Logs to Recipe Cards tab
Option C
🧬 DNA Flavor Pairing Lab
Culinary_DNAFlavor.html
  • Word Lab — OneWord → hero ingredient → surprise pairings
  • Pairing Test — pick 2 ingredients → compatibility score
  • Science Lab — molecular gastronomy science report per word
  • Surprise Lab — pick a food → 3 shocking pairings revealed
  • Prints flavor pairing card · Logs to DNA Flavor Lab tab
Option D
💻 Bake the Code
Culinary_BakeTheCode.html
  • Student picks their OneWord → sees a 4-letter binary code
  • Decodes each letter using ASCII reference chart
  • Cookie decorates live as each letter is solved
  • Free-paint coloring canvas: Round or Word shape
  • Prints result card + coloring sheet
  • Logs to Bake the Code tab

🎨 Play-Doh Activity Card
The Play-Doh card is the child-facing piece of the Culinary contribution. The cookie cutter is meaningful to the high schooler who chose it — but the Play-Doh card is what makes the bag magical for a 5-year-old.
What the Card Should Include
Each student creates a card with 2–3 play ideas for their cookie cutter word — some using the cutter with Play-Doh, some without. Example for Bloom:

With Play-Doh: Press your Bloom cutter into the dough to make a flower garden!
Without Play-Doh: Trace the cutter on paper and color your flower in your favorite colors.
Without Play-Doh: Use the cutter as a stencil and paint over it with watercolors!

Cards should be simple, joyful, and written for a young child to understand. The student's name should appear as "Made for you by [First Name]."
How to Run This Activity
1
Student completes the Cookie Cutter Order kiosk (Option A) first to confirm their word.
2
Student writes their 2–3 play ideas on the provided card template.
3
Card is printed or hand-written, laminated if possible, and placed in the Smile Bag alongside the cookie cutter and Play-Doh.
4
Each bag contains: cookie cutter + Play-Doh + activity card = the complete Culinary Arts contribution.
🚀 Running the Kiosk in Class
1
Project or share the hub URL — lhhowell.github.io/kellamsmilebags/Culinary_Menu.html — students open it on a shared device or their own.
2
All students must complete Option A (Cookie Cutter Order) — this is what generates the print queue for you.
3
Options B, C, and D are choice activities — students pick one (or more) to explore and print their card.
4
After class — open the Cookie Cutter Orders spreadsheet, sort by color, and batch your Bambu prints.
5
Assemble bags — printed cutter + Play-Doh + student's activity card go into each bag together.
