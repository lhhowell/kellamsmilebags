What You're Making

You are creating an original inspirational quote card for a child receiving a Smile Bag. Your quote will be printed on a 4-inch card in your chosen colors and font, and placed in the bag by the Operation Smiles Club. The child receiving your card may be going through something hard. Your words — connected to one of the 25 OneWords — can make a real difference.

✓Your OneWord displayed at the top
✓Your original inspirational quote in the center
✓Your first name at the bottom
✓Your chosen colors and font style
✓Shape of your choice — 4″ square or 4″ circle

The 8 Steps

1
Choose Your Shape

Square: 4″ × 4″ card — clean and bold  |  Circle: 4″ round card — soft and great for displaying

2
Enter Your Name & Grade

Type your first name. It will appear on the finished card. Select your grade from the dropdown.

3
Choose Your OneWord

Browse all 25 OneWords and their meanings. Choose the word that speaks to you most. This word will be the heart of your quote.

4
Reflect — This is the most important step!

Complete a Quick Write — 2 to 3 sentences answering: What does your word mean to you? When have you seen this word come alive in real life? What would this word mean to a child who is struggling right now?

Then open the Gemini Writing Tutor using the link your teacher shares. The tutor will ask you questions — it will NOT write the quote for you. Answer honestly. The tutor helps you go deeper, not easier.

5
Write Your Quote

Enter your final quote — refined through your tutor conversation. Maximum 120 characters. Shorter is stronger. Use 'you' language — speak directly to the child. Your word should appear in or clearly connect to the quote.

6
Choose Your Color Pair

Color 1 = card background  |  Color 2 = text and border color. All colors are based on school color palette.

7
Choose Your Font

Elegant — Playfair Display (classic and formal)  ·  Friendly — Fredoka One (rounded and warm)  ·  Script — Satisfy (handwritten feel)  ·  Bold — Oswald (strong and powerful)

8
Preview & Submit

See your finished card with your name, word, quote, colors, and font. When you are happy with it, click Submit My Quote Card! You will receive a KSB-XXXX code — write it down as your receipt. Ms. Vinson will print and cut your card. No printing needed from you!

What Makes a Great Quote?

✓ Strong Quotes

  • Speak directly to the child using 'you'
  • Connect deeply to the OneWord's meaning
  • Are original and true to you
  • Are short and powerful — every word counts

✗ Weak Quotes

  • Could have been written by anyone
  • Are generic ("Be happy" · "Never give up")
  • Don't connect to the OneWord
  • Sound like they were written by AI
Example — Word: BREATHE
Weak"Always remember to breathe and stay calm."
Strong"When everything feels like too much — just breathe. You've gotten through 100% of your hard days."

Costa's Levels of Thinking

Use these levels to push your thinking deeper before writing your quote.

LevelQuestion to Answer
Level 1 — Recall What does your word mean? What is its definition?
Level 2 — Connection When have you seen this word come alive in real life? Give a specific example.
Level 3 — Application What would this word mean to a child who is struggling right now? How could it help them?
Your quote should live at Level 3 — applying the word as wisdom for someone who needs it.
