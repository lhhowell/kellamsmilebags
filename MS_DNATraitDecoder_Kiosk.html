The DNA Recipe Code

DNA is made of four chemical bases — Adenine (A), Thymine (T), Guanine (G), and Cytosine (C) — that always bond in specific pairs: A with T, and G with C. This pairing rule is the same in every living thing on Earth, from bacteria to blue whales. In our code, four food ingredients represent these four bases. Decode the ingredient pairs to reveal the hidden OneWord — the same way a cell reads a strand of DNA!

🍅 T = Thymine (Tomato) always pairs with A
🍇 G = Guanine (Grape) always pairs with C
🥕 C = Cytosine (Carrot) always pairs with G

🧬 The Biology Connection
DNA is found in the nucleus of every cell in your body. It is made of four chemical bases — Adenine, Thymine, Guanine, and Cytosine — that always pair the same way: A with T, G with C. This pairing rule never changes — not in humans, not in plants, not in bacteria. The ORDER of these base pairs determines your traits: eye color, blood type, even how tall you grow. A single change in one base pair is called a mutation — sometimes harmless, sometimes the cause of disease. You just decoded a message using the same base pairing rules that nature has used for 3.5 billion years!
