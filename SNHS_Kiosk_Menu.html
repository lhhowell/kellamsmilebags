Smile Bags · A Word & Wonder Experience

SNHS Creation Kiosk

Choose 10 items from the menu below across at least 3 categories to build one complete Smile Bag for a middle school student. Every item must be an original creation made with technology — except one.

🌿 10 Items Required
⭐ 5 Service Points
🏫 Middle School Recipients
🚀 March — May 2026
← Back to SNHS Overview

How to Use This Menu

Browse all four categories below. Select 10 items total spanning at least 3 categories. Items marked with a dashed border are student choice — sponsor approved — bring your idea to your sponsor before you begin. All items must be digitally designed and printed unless otherwise noted. Print size is 5×7 unless specified.

📌 All supplies and materials listed in this menu are provided through the project. Any materials not listed are the student's responsibility to obtain.

🔬 Science & Inquiry
Items 1–5
1
Short Story — OneWord as the Character
Write an original short story where your OneWord is the main character. The story should reflect both the environmental science meaning and the social-emotional meaning of your word. Digitally designed and printed. Illustrated cover encouraged.
Science & Inquiry5×7 or folded booklet
2
Science Supply Bar + Bookmark Builder Kiosk
Visit the Science Supply Bar and choose 1 or 2 supplies. Use the Bookmark Builder Kiosk to design 1 bookmark that connects your supply choice to your OneWord through scientific reasoning and vocabulary. Your bookmark + your supplies go into the bag together. If a supply you want is not on the bar, your sponsor must approve it in advance.
Science & InquiryBookmark + 1–2 supplies
3
Science Fact Card — “Did You Know?”
Design an original “Did You Know?” fact card connected to your OneWord. Include one surprising science fact, a simple diagram or illustration, and one sentence connecting the fact to social-emotional well-being. Digitally designed and printed.
Science & Inquiry5×7
4
Science Vocabulary Trading Card
Design a trading card for your OneWord featuring the scientific definition, the SEL definition, an illustration, and one real-world example. Front and back design. Printed on cardstock and cut to size.
Science & Inquiry3×4 trading card
5
Science Comic Strip
Create an original comic strip (3–6 panels) where a character experiences your OneWord in a real environmental science context. The story should be relatable for a middle schooler and connect science to everyday life. Digitally designed and printed.
