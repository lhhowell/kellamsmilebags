At Kellam High School, Virginia Teachers for Tomorrow and English 12 seniors are crafting one-of-a-kind items for students at their own school. These bags carry something no other class can offer — words written by someone who knows exactly what it feels like to be where you are. A graduating senior made this for you. On purpose. With care.

At the heart of Smile Bags are 25 OneWords — connecting environmental science to social emotional well-being.

Each of the 25 OneWords lives in two worlds at once — in the natural systems students study, and in the inner lives students navigate every day. From Absorb to Transform, these words remind us that the same forces that sustain ecosystems are the forces that sustain people too.

Who Receives a Smile Bag

A Kellam student made this
for another Kellam student.

The bags from Robin Hoffman's classes go to Kellam High School students — ten bags, identified by guidance counselors. These students may be navigating something hard. They may need encouragement, motivation, or simply a reason to smile. A bag is chosen for them deliberately, not randomly — at exactly the right moment.

Every item inside was made by a graduating senior who didn't know your name — but made something just for you.

A Legacy Left Behind

Many of Robin Hoffman's students are graduating this spring. These Smile Bags are one of their final acts at Kellam — a chance to leave something meaningful for the students who come after them. The words they write, the items they make, and the care they put in will outlast their time here.
